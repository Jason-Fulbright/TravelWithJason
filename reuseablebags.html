Invest in Grocery Bags

Jason Fulbright

10 Aug, 2019 11:21 am

Invest in bags you ask??? Grocery bags carry very little economic value so this is not a financial investment but rather an investment into your future, your children's future, and the world's fate.

Hopefully that got your attention because this is an issue that can no longer be ignored as it is now estimated that by 2050 plastics in the ocean will outweigh fish in the ocean. To get an idea of how much plastic that is, its the equivalent of dumping one garbage truck full of trash into the ocean every minute. Yeah that's a lot and it adds up quick.

Humans have began using excessive amounts of plastics, in fact we are now using 20 times the amount of plastic compared to 50 years ago. However, using plastics isn't the main issue, its using plastics only once that is the issue.

Today we use plastic - a material designed to last forever - for products designed to last minutes. UPSTREAM
Plastics last nearly forever, exactly why we don't want them in our environment, so we must reuse as many plastics as we can. This means reusing plastic containers, recycling old plastics, and reducing your single-use plastic consumption.

What's a good way to start reducing your intake of single use plastics? Reusable grocery bag! Everyone goes to the grocery store at least once a week to restock the fridge and pantry. You pick out your meals for the week and snag a few of your favorite snacks and go to check out. Upon checking out the cashier bags your groceries, you take them to your car, and then leave for home. When you get home you un-bag your groceries and fill the pantry. The grocery bags life is now over, lasting a total of 15 minutes or however long it takes you to get the to store. This bag will now be thrown away and taken to a landfill. At the landfill it will likely blow away because it is very light and many of these bags will never even make it to the dump because they blow out of dump trucks and trash bins so easily. This bag will now start its journey to the ocean. Being easily carried by wind and water, most plastic bags eventually find their way into the ocean where they will now float around waiting to be mistakenly eaten by sea turtles (they eat jellyfish which look a lot like plastic bags), choked on by whales(see this National Geographic video of just that), or entangled in a number of other marine species.

So lets stop all of that from happening and purchase a few reusable grocery bags. These cost $3 each, can carry a TON of groceries, last literally forever, won't tear and drop all of your groceries, and are easier to use compared to flimsy plastic bags.

So what's stopping you? Get out there and start saving the planet one grocery trip at a time!
