The Digs of This Iconic City

My trip details
  • Duration: 1 day
  • Time Frame: Mid June
  • Locations: Touro and French Quarter

Summary

Nola could take the cake for be my favorite city in the United States, and the food might be why...

Weather

It was a beautiful day when I visited and this region gets pretty great weather since it is surrounded by water which helps cool the air during hot summers and regulate cold spells during the mild winters.

Food

The food in New Orleans is to die for. You are always within a mile, if not blocks, of a delicious restaurant including countless creole digs, amazing delis, and fresh seafood dives. I tried Cochon a terrific deli located along the South side of the French Quarter and got an awesome muffuletta sandwich, had a tasty hurricane at Pat'O Brien's, got jambalaya and beignets at Café Beignet and finished the day with a delicious daiquiri.

The Locals

Super friendly people both visiting and the those who lived there!

Driving Conditions

The driving conditions in New Orleans were not bad at all considering it is a major city. Parking was easy to find and drivers on the road were quite friendly when compared to many other U.S. cities.

Safeness

I never had any safety concern during my trip to Nola and doubt you will too.

Priceyness

Things here were very moderately priced, especially considering the quality of food and service you recived. In fact I would say it was a bit underpriced for what you got.

Transportation

One of my favorite ways to get around cities is trollies and New Orleans has a terrific trolley system that will take you all over the city for a reasonable price. Be sure to try it out!

Touristyness

Even though this is a city visited by people from all over I did not feel any pressure from locals to buy things or even see many tourist trap kinda shops. It was actually quite a relief when I found that this city was untouched by the tourist trap plague which you find in many gulf coast cities.

Nightlife

Unfortunately I did not have a chance to experience the night life of this amazing city but I have heard it is truly awesome and I can believe after seeing Bourbon street quite full at only 5pm .

Essential stops

I highly recommend checking out Couchon and Café Beignet for some awesome food and Pat'O Brien's for drinks, other than that I would suggest walking the streets surrounding Jefferson Square and finding a bar or restaurant to relax and people watch.
