How to Travel Europe(and everywhere else) Cheap

Jason Fulbright

19 Aug ,2019 8:00 am

So like most of us we love to travel but don't have all the money in the world to do it with so we gotta travel cheap, well not necessarily cheap but economically. So this article will help you get more out of your hard earned dollars when you're exploring the world.

Jobs fill your pockets, adventures fill the soul. Wise Travelers

Plane Tickets

First we have to get there so were is the best place to buy your plane tickets. (And this is important since your tickets will likely be a large portion of your travel expenses)

I highly recommend SkyScanner.com to buy plane tickets that require multiple stops. You'll find tickets for less than half the cost on other websites because of SkyScanner's unique approach to ticket buying. The way it works is SkyScanner searches every airline for combinations of flights that will get you where your going. So instead of flying American airline and it's partner airlines from LA to London, SkyScanner may book an American airline ticket from LA to Chicago and then a separate Air France ticket from Chicago to London saving you hundreds! Check it out the next time you are booking flights.

When booking flights that are non-stop I like Google Flights. Google's flight search engine looks at hundreds of other websites and finds the best price for you and then you book your flight through either the airline or the other website that google referenced like Expedia. Another cool feature of Google Flights is its price calendar. This shows the different prices for the same flight on each day so if you are flexible and can travel on a Wednesday instead of a Friday it can save you a ton! A similar but I think less useful feature is the nearby airports option. This allows you to compare the cost of flights to a general area instead of to and from specific airports.

Ground Transportation

Okay, so we got you there now how are we going to get around? One of my favorite options for transport is trains, yes it might sound a bit 1980 but trains are cheap, fast, and quite comfortable. In fact, nowadays they are really cheap and some are REALLY fast! I bought a EuRail pass for my trip to Europe which cost only $350(normally $450 for adults but travelers under 27 get a HUGE discount) for 10 travel days within a two month time frame. This means I can hop on and off of the trains during a 24hr period on 10 different days! I'm using it to get quite literally all across Europe (Paris to Amsterdam with a day trip in Belgium along the way, Amsterdam to Berlin, Berlin to Prague, Prague to Munich, Munich to Budapest, Budapest to Vienna, Vienna to Florence, Florence to Pompeii for the day and then back to Rome, Rome to Marseille, Marseille to Barcelona) Yeah, all that for $350!!!

Other cheap and rather enjoyable transport methods include subways and trollies, so really rail is the way to go if you are traveling big cities. However, not everywhere has a well established rail system. In places that lack this I turn to Lift and Uber if it is available otherwise taxies are about your only option outside of renting a vehicle.

Accommodations

This is where things can get a bit tricky since there are sooo many options for places to stay and too many places to look for them, but these are my recommendations for finding a great place to lay your head while on your adventures. Trivago is a website that searches thousands of other sites for available rooms in any city you can think of. This is great for finding places to stay during high seasons when many things are booked full. However, I have found that other websites like Booking.com and Expedia will have exclusive deals on places that won't show up in a Trivago search so I always like to check them after search Trivago to see if they offer any nice deals. If hotels and hostels aren't what you are looking for or if they are all booked up(Octoberfest in Munich for example) then I love Airbnb. Airbnb can be more expensive for short stays due to service and booking fees but I encourage you to check it out when looking for your next place to stay.
