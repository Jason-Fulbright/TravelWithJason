Jason's Humble Beginning

I grew up in central Arkansas and started my education at a small school in the rural South called Bismarck. Classes were fun, I had great friends, and I loved sports, especially football. Even though I was in a landlocked state it was around this time that my obsession with travel and the oceans started. My family has been blessed enough to be able to travel to the Gulf Coast, Caribbean, or the Pacific every year since I was born, and those travels have fostered a love for the beach, warm weather, and blue water within me. Since I was still quite young at this time, I didn’t yet understand the problems that surrounded me, but as I grew older, I began to learn of issues such as climate change, plastic pollution, and ocean acidification. After learning these things and seeing the harm humanity was causing to our precious waters, and my love for the ocean grew even stronger and I began to look for ways in which I could motivate people to love the ocean as I do and encourage everyone to take care of the oceans as they take care of us. However, my education at Bismarck was not preparing me to inspire those changes.

Fortunately, my parents and I stumbled across an opportunity that would change my life. We learned of the Arkansas School for Mathematics, Sciences, and the Arts, a selective high school that prepares students for college. I applied and was accepted; however, this was just the first step to a long journey. Moving to ASMSA made me realize I had been living in a bubble. Unlike Bismarck, ASMSA had a variety of people from across the state each with their own language, music, food, clothes, and beliefs. This was my first chance to truly meet people from a variety of other cultures, and I found that you can never actually understand someone until you learn and interact with their culture. Because of this we must strive to expose ourselves to other cultures and others to ours, since it is through cultural intermingling that we meet new people, form stronger bonds, inspire smarter ideas, and work towards a more peaceful world. But that was only half of the ASMSA experience, this is where I learned about the currents of the oceans, the forces behind gravity, the code within programs, and even conducted a year and a half research experiment that placed in the state science fair. ASMSA ultimately taught me the importance of broadening my horizons, meeting new people, experiencing new things, looking past the norm, questioning why things are, and this is what lead me to Miami. I had never been to Miami before the day I moved into the dorm and that’s one of the main reasons I decided to go there. It was yet another new world for me to experience, learn from, and build upon just as I hope to do in Barbados. And after my four years at UMiami I decided it was time to continue my explorations elsewhere.

I have had the chance to travel around the world and those journeys have opened my eyes to the issues which escape the news. I have seen Greece in a time of economic crisis, I have witnessed the drastic differences between small town Mexico and the busy tourist packed cities and how that effects everyday life, and I have experienced the Galapagos in a time of drastic change with the sudden shift to tourism and the issues, like pollution, it has created. These travels have made me aware that the world continues to go on even when you are not watching. So I hope to make all of us more in touch with what is going on within us, around us, and beyond us because as things change so we must constantly adapt with them.

If I'm an advocate for anything, it's to move....walk in someone else's shoes or at least eat their food, it's a plus for everybody. Anthony Bourdain
