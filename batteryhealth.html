How to Keep Your Phone Battery Lasting All Day

Jason Fulbright

28 Aug ,2019 5:00 pm

One of the biggest reasons we upgrade to a new phone is because the old one's battery just doesn't last like it did when we got it. We all remember our iPhone that would die at 30% after using it for a few hours, so how do you prevent this from happening?

Keep that phone battery lasting longer than today's relationships Jason Fulbright

Don't Let it Die

Unlike older batteries, Lithium Ion batteries should not be completely discharged with every use. Older batteries suffered from something called battery memory which would cause them to have a reduced capacity if they were not completely discharged and fully charged each time. However, Lithium batteries suffer from almost the opposite problem. A certain voltage needs to be kept in these batteries in order for them to charge properly and prevent deterioration so try to keep you phone from dying completely before you charge it. However, there is one exception to this. Every 30 charges, about once a month, you should allow it to completely die in order for the electronics of the phone to reset the battery percentage gauge but other than this try not to let it get to 0%.

Heat is the Enemy

Lithium batteries do not enjoy getting hot, this is one of the main factors that cause new batteries to deteriorate and not last as long as they did new. So be sure to keep you phone from sitting in the sun or in a hot car for too long. If your phone does get hot try to let it cool down before using to prevent further harm.

Charge in Sessions

Your phone battery also gets hot while you are charging it, so instead of charging you phone all at once it is better to do it in sessions and not to leave it on the charger after it has fully charged. I find its best to put it on the charger in the evenings(during dinner or while watching tv), take it off while you sleep, and put it back on the charger while you are getting ready in the morning to top it off before starting your day.

Storage

If you plan on not using you phone for a while but want the battery to stay healthy while just sitting it is recommended to have it charged to around 40% before setting it down and storing it in a cool dry place.

I hope these tips have been helpful and you can keep you phone battery lasting all day!
