A Warm Welcoming to Europe

My trip details
  • Duration: 4 days
  • Time Frame: Early Sept
  • Location: Paris

Summary

Paris is one of the most historic and beautiful places I have ever been. From the old style architecture to the delicious cuisine, I was falling in love so if you ever don't hear from me, I've probably run away to Paris.

Weather

During my stay I experienced nothing but beautiful weather. During the day it was warm enough to wear shorts and shirts around but as the sun went down a jacket was needed. I would highly recommend visting Paris during this time as the weather was perfect for walking around all day and since it is at the beginning of the low season there are not as many tourist so it is easier to check out the main attractions(ie the Louvre, Arc de Triomphe, Eiffel Tower, ect)

Food

There was always good food to be found in this city and you never have to go far to get it. Everywhere I ate at in Paris was great but the pricing varied greatly. Getting food outside of the main tourist areas was much cheaper than eating say near the Eiffel Tower but the quality of the regular restaurants remained about the same all around.

The Locals

Almost everyone in Paris was super helpful and friendly, and many of them spoke English so getting around without French was no problem. However, as with many big cities you have the issue of pickpockets. I recommend when you travel to Paris or any other major city be aware of this and keep your valuables close. Unlike the in the United States, you need to keep your phone and wallet in either a bag or in your front pockets to help avoid having them taken.

Driving Conditions

Unlike what you might hear the drivers in Europe are just as safe as those in the states, if not safer. I didn't see any wrecks while in Paris and never felt uncomfortable when biking on the roads. But if you decided to rent a car know that finding parking in any city, whether it is Miami or Paris parking spots are few and far between so I suggest using the public transportation methods (Busses and the Subway) to go far distances or renting bikes/scooters for short ones. Something that is extremely fun is the electric scooter rentals that you can pick up and drop off where ever. These rentals start a $1 and increase by 20 Cents every minute.

Safeness

I never felt any uneasiness during my trip in Paris. However, I was at the bar with some friends and one of them had their wallet stolen which ruined the night but this was the worst thing that happened around me during my stay.

Priceyness

Paris was slightly less expensive when compared to major cities in the states, like Miami or LA. Most nice sit down dinners costed around $12-15 for the main course and getting drinks while out was quite a bit cheaper with it being around $5 for a glass of wine or $4 for a beer.

Transportation

Paris has a great subway system so use it! It cost $1.90 to go from any part of the city to another and it is quite quick. The subways do close late at night so after going out you may have to take the buses but this cost the same, its just much slower.

Touristyness

Paris was very touristy. You will constantly find people trying to sell you knick-knacks but remember you don't need to buy everything that's offered to you and you can always walk away. If you do want to buy something from a street vendor it is good to know that they will typically bargain with you. I forgot my sunglasses one day and bought a cheap pair from someone on the street. He was asking $12 for them but after some haggling I got them for $5.

Nightlife

The nightlife of Paris was great but with so many clubs you might have to do a little looking around to find one that is full if you are going out on a weekday. If you can't find a particular club you would like to go to check out the Latin Quarter. This areas is covered in bars and small clubs that always have something going on!

Essential stops

I highly recommend going to the Sacre-Coeur right before sunset to check out the beautiful church and its breathtaking stained glass windows in the afternoon sunlight. After that hang out on the steps to the church for a drink and live music all while overlooking the city. I also loved the Louvre and spent almost an entire day there, and if you are up for some stairs climb to the top of the Arc de Triomphe for an amazing view of the city.
