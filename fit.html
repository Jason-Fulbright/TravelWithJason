The Basics of Staying Healthy

Jason Fulbright

27 Aug, 2019 11:00 am

Staying healthy is an issue many of us are facing today and with more and more "healthy" options out there it is important to know what actually helps.

The first wealth is health. Ralph Waldo Emerson

Full meals

Let's start with what you eat, or actually how much you eat. Full meals are more important than you think. Having a well-rounded and good-sized breakfast, lunch, and dinner is super important in a healthy lifestyle. This is where you get the energy and nutrients that you need to power through your day. Instead of having little or no breakfast and being hungry through the day (and likely snacking to compensate) have a full breakfast that will give you the calories you need to work hard and have a good day. And continue having full meals for lunch and dinner to avoid excessive snacking. You typically eat more through the day when you have smaller meals because your body can consume the food quicker(more room in your stomach) and you become hungry much sooner calorie for calorie than if you had a full meal, this is actually a method body builders use to gain weight.

Don't Eat Too Late

We are all busy and work until we are ready to pass out but it is important to not leave your dinner as one of the last things you do before bed. Eating soon before bed has been found to induce heartburn and even weight gain. Try to eat 2-3 hours before bed in order to allow enough time for you body to digest your dinner before laying down. This will actually improve your sleep as well! When you eat right before going to be you body stays up since your stomach is still working to digest, keeping you from getting a deep-restful sleep.

Eat Healthy Snacks

We all snack throughout the day but what you snack on maters more than you think. Even though you may only have a few chips or crackers at a time, you'll do this multiple times a day adding up to a lot of empty calories. So instead of reaching for Doritos grab some of your favorite fruits, nuts, or veggies to fill up on between meals. This will fill you up with more nutrients and less calories than processed food, and I feel healthier and more alert after having a natural snack when compared to a processed one.

Be Active Everyday

Whether its taking the dog for a walk, yoga with the friends, or a full out gym sesh try to be active at least a little everyday. This will get you into a habit of being active and make you feel better after getting your blood flowing. In fact, during exercise you body releases anandamide. Anandamide, know as the bliss molecule is a neurotransmitter that stimulates the endocannabinoid system which reduces anxiety and increases the feeling of happiness. Sound familiar? This is the same system that is activated by smoking pot but don't try to replace exercise with a smoke sesh as it sadly won't have the same effect.

Drink Water

Last but definitely not least, drink plenty of water! If you are trying to lose weight, drinking water instead of juice or soda cuts hundreds of calories from your diet a day. Water also is involved in nearly every function of your body. It aids in the digestion of food, regulates body temperature, helps remove toxins, assists brain function, lubricates and cushions joints, aids in nutrient absorption, and can help to prevent hangovers! So be sure to drink water, from you reusable water bottle I hope, through out the day!
