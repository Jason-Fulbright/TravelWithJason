Why Choose Fluoride-Free

Jason Fulbright

12 Dec ,2017 11:21 am

When buying a new toothpaste there are soo many options, do I get a whitening toothpaste, does it need fragments of breath strips in it, should it be a gel or paste, and do I need fluoride??? Many of these questions can be ignored as they are just marketing schemes to sell more expensive toothpaste but the issue of fluoride is important.

To begin, what even is fluoride? Fluoride is a naturally occurring mineral typically found in rocks and trace amounts can be found in large bodies of water. The discovery of fluoride's ability to protect teeth occurred by accident in the early 1900's. Dentist were called to Colorado Springs to examine a mysterious aliment.

Know as Colorado Brown Stain this disorder caused Colorado Springs' youth to have permanent brown stains on their teeth, but surprisingly these stained teeth were found to be quite resistant to decay. After years of testing water from towns across the country where this was happening, scientist finally discovered that it was naturally occurring(simi-naturally- likely increased by mining in these areas) fluoride in the towns' water supplies causing this discoloration. The towns then found new water supplies and the problem was solved.

Years later, another dentist read the reports of this study and wanted to test if using fluoride in small amounts would strengthen teeth since they noted that the stained teeth were less susceptible to decay. He got his wish and the city of Grand Rapids, Michigan decided to add fluoride to its public water supply the following year. In 1945, Grand Rapids became the first city in the world to fluoridate its drinking water. After just 11 years, they announced an amazing finding. The cavity rate among Grand Rapids children born after fluoride was added to the water supply dropped more than 60 percent which is great, but what they didn't study was the effects of this mineral on brain development. Despite not knowing the entire effects of fluoride, cities across the country began to fluorinate their water and now nearly 70% of U.S. water supplies are fluorinated.

Our very great concern is that children worldwide are being exposed to unrecognized toxic chemicals that are silently eroding intelligence, disrupting behaviors, truncating future achievements, and damaging societies, perhaps most seriously in developing countries. Harvard scientist Philippe Grandjea

What is Dr. Grandjea talking about? Now we have a better ability to examine the health effects of chemicals on humans. Through these studies we have found a list of chemicals that cause problems with children's brains known as developmental neurotoxicants. This list includes lead, methylmercury, arsenic, manganese, and guess who, fluoride. Just like fluoride, lead was once thought to be safe and was used in a variety of products around the house but we found out that it was a neurotoxin and stopped its use. Like lead, now over 400 studies have found that fluoride is a neurotoxin with 53 of these studies linking moderately high fluoride exposures to reduced intelligence.

So why is there still fluoride in our water and toothpaste? One reason is because community water fluoridation has ben shown to save money for the U.S. health care system. Instead of treating individuals for cavities its easier and cheaper to treat everyone but that is exactly the reason most Western European countries have ruled against fluoridation. Government should not have the power to force individuals to take medication without their consent. On top of this, the dosage is not controlled since everyone drinks different amounts of water everyday, fluoride is given to everyone regardless of their age, health, or vulnerability, there is no such thing as fluoride deficiency since fluoride is not an essential nutrient, and it is now been found that tooth decay has dropped equally in countries that fluoridate their water and those who don't. Plus many other reasons to oppose fluoridation here.

But it will likely be a very long time before water fluoridation stops in the U.S., so how can you reduce your fluoride intake to help protect your brain? I suggest choosing a fluoride free toothpaste such as these (hello, Tom's of Maine, Burt's Bees), if you forget your fluoride-free toothpaste and must use regular toothpaste rinse your mouth out thoroughly after brushing to keep from swallowing any extra fluoride, and try to drink from fluoride-free water supplies.
